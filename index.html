About Me

With a passion for all things engineering, Alex enjoys finding that ideal solution that considers the whole. From studying the original system of the environment to the languages behind the digital world, Alex uses a variety of skills and experiences to engineer that solution. With an entrepreneurial spirit, he looks to build upon these diverse skills and find areas that can continue to challenge him.

A Lancaster, PA, native, Alex went from saxophone player to rower at Lehigh University, when he joined the class of 2013. When not rowing, Alex studied for a major in Environmental Engineering and also found a minor in science and environmental reporting. Staying at Lehigh for another two years, Alex continued studying environmental engineering to achieve a Master’s degree and researched novel waste to energy processes. This work led to a stint consulting with an energy startup and even travelling to Turkey to set up a R&D lab there. Pivoting from the excitement of a startup, Alex joined the Army Public Health Command in 2015. Serving as an Army civilian, Alex consulted and led a wide variety of environmental projects with a focus on field water and water reuse technologies. In December of 2018, Alex became a professional engineer in the state of Maryland.

Currently, Alex is expanding his knowledge in a newfound passion for coding. Still rowing, but now with Baltimore Community Rowing and the men’s competitive team, he aslso tried to find time to hike, ski, play games, or just relax by the river. His vice is older BMWs of which at any given time may or may not be running. Given the time, he will also make custom vinyl stickers as a creative outlet. Alex currently resides in Baltimore City with his fiance, Alyssa, and mini Irish-doodle, Moose.
